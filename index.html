Rancho Deep Cliff HOA · April 2026 · For all homeowners

A new architectural restriction could permanently close the door on adding a 3rd bedroom for many homes in our community.

Community First Property Management recently sent out a notice about a proposed change to our architectural guidelines. If it got lost in your inbox, please read on — what is being proposed has real implications for your home and your ability to make changes to it in the future.

What is being proposed

A new architectural restriction has been added to the HOA's Non-Standard Architectural Modification guidelines under "Street Facing Window Alterations." It reads:

"Replaced windows must fit in the same wall cut-outs as the existing window and the windowpane configuration cannot be altered. The wall cut-out size cannot be altered, and the cut-out cannot be relocated on the wall."

In plain terms: no street-facing window can ever be enlarged, not now and not in the future, regardless of how carefully the work is designed or how well it matches the surrounding homes.

California Residential Code Section R319 requires every legally recognized bedroom to have an emergency egress window meeting the dimensions above. In several RDC floor plans, the only exterior wall available for a bedroom conversion contains a small bathroom window that does not meet these requirements. Enlarging it is the only path forward, and this restriction closes that path permanently.
This affects every homeowner, not just those planning renovations

Young families who outgrow two bedrooms will have no option but to leave RDC, unable to grow into their own home

Prospective buyers with growing families will see this restriction and choose a community that gives them room to grow

No space for a caregiver, a returning adult child, or grandchildren staying for extended visits

Resale value and long-term property appeal permanently capped for every homeowner in the community

The justification given
  • "It has never been done before since the builder built them"
  • "Duet homes should be mirror images"

The board has already approved window pane changes and color differences that make duet homes visually asymmetrical. Saying something has not been done before is a description of history, not a reason to restrict the future. Our CC&Rs require that architectural standards be reasonable and objective, not arbitrary (Article VIII, Section 8.01).

Our community already has variation
  • Different window sizes across units
  • Different sliding door sizes
  • Different elevations
  • Different floor plans

These differences exist today and the neighborhood still looks cohesive and harmonious. Variation and visual consistency have always coexisted here, and there is no reason a carefully reviewed window modification would change that.

Our community has always balanced consistency with good judgment. A thoughtful, case-by-case review process would serve everyone far better than a permanent prohibition. We are not asking for unrestricted changes. We are asking that the door not be permanently shut on a reasonable, well-designed modification that some homeowners may genuinely need.
